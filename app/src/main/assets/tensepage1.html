Past Tense

It can be further divided into four categories.


Past Indefinite Tense:

simple sentence

These tenses end with dita,kita and liya in Punjabi and kiya, liya and diya in hindi.

RULES:

2nd form of verb

Example:
In English: Ram went to school
In Punjabi: Ram School chala gya
In Hindi: Ram school chala gya

Negative sentence

RULES:

Did not + first form of verb

Example:
In English: Ram did not go to school.
In Punjabi: Ram school nahi gya.
In Hindi: Ram school nahi gya

Interrogative sentence

RULES:

Did before noun + first form of verb

Example:
In English: Did ram go to school?
In punjabi: ki Ram school gya.
In Hindi: Kya Ram school gya.


Past continuous Tense

simple sentence

Ends with reha c, rahi c and rahe c and rha tha, rahi thi in hindi.

RULES:

was / were + 1st form of verb + ing

  • was - singular
  • were - plural

Example:
In English: Ram was going to school
In Punjabi: Ram school ja reha c
In Hindi: Ram school ja rha tha.

Negative sentence

RULES:

: was not / were not + 1st form of verb + ing

  • was not - singular
  • were not- plural

Example:
In English: Ram was not going to school.
In Punjabi: Ram school nahi ja reha c.
In Hindi: Ram school nahi ja rha tha.

Interrogative sentence

RULES:

was/were before noun + 1st form of verb + ing

Example:
In English: was ram going to school?
In punjabi: ki Ram school ja reha c?
In Hindi: Kya Ram school ja rha tha?


Present perfect Tense

simple sentence

Ends with dita c, kita c and liya c in Punjabi and kiya tha, liya tha and diya tha in hindi.

RULES:

: had + 3rd form of verb

Example:
In English: Ram had gone to school.
In Punjabi: Ram school chala gya c.
In Hindi: Ram school chala gya tha.

Negative sentence

RULES:

: had not + 3st form of verb

Example:
In English:: Ram had not gone to school.
In Punjabi: Ram school nahi gya c.
In Hindi: Ram school nahi gya tha.

Interrogative sentence

RULES:

had before noun + 3st form of verb

Example:
In English: Had ram gone to school?
In punjabi: ki Ram school chala gya c?
In Hindi: Kya Ram school chala gya tha?


Present perfect continuous Tense

simple sentence

Ends with reha c, rahi c and rahe san same as in past continuous. But the only difference is mentioning of time from which the action is being performed.

  • for: In case of exact time
  • since: Not exact time

RULES:

had been + 1st form of verb + ing

Example:
In English: Ram had been going to school since 3 years
In Punjabi: Ram 3 sala to school ja reha c.
In Hindi: Ram 3 saal se school ja rha tha.

Negative sentence

RULES:

: had not been + 3st form of verb

Example:
In English:: Ram had not been going to school since 3 years
In Punjabi: Ram 3 sala to school nahi ja reha c.
In Hindi: Ram 3 saal se school nahi ja rha tha.

Interrogative sentence

RULES:

had before noun + been + 1st form of verb +ing

Example:
In English: Had Ram been going to school since 3 years
In punjabi: ki Ram 3 sala to school ja reha c?
In Hindi: Kya Ram 3 salon se school ja rha tha?
