Present Tense

It can be further divided into four categories.


Present Indefinite Tense:

simple sentence

These tenses end with da hai, di hai and de han in Punjabi and ta hai, ti hai in hindi.

RULES:

For Singular: 1st form of verb + s or es

For Plural: 1st form of verb

  • Use s if verb ends with vowel
  • Use es if verb ends with consonant

Example:
In English: Ram goes to school
In Punjabi: Ram School janda hai
In Hindi: Ram school jata hai

Negative sentence

RULES:

Do not or Does not + first form of verb

  • Do not is used with plural
  • Does not is used with singular

Example:
In English: Ram does not go to school.
In Punjabi: Ram school nahi janda hai.
In Hindi: Ram school nahi jata hai

Interrogative sentence

RULES:

Do or does before noun + first form of verb

  • Do for plural
  • Does for singular

Example:
In English: Does ram go to school?
In punjabi: ki Ram school janda hai.
In Hindi: Kya Ram school jata hai.


Present continuous Tense

simple sentence

Ends with reha hai, rahi hai and rahe han.

RULES:

: is / are / am + 1st form of verb + ing

  • Is - singular
  • are - plural
  • am - I

Example:
In English: Ram is going to school
In Punjabi: Ram school ja reha hai
In Hindi: Ram school ja rha hai.

Negative sentence

RULES:

: is not / am not / are not + 1st form of verb + ing

  • Is not - singular
  • are not- plural
  • am not- I

Example:
In English: Ram is not going to school.
In Punjabi: Ram school nahi ja reha hai
In Hindi: Ram school nahi ja rha hai

Interrogative sentence

RULES:

Is/am/are before noun + 1st form of verb + ing

Example:
In English: Is ram going to school?
In punjabi: ki Ram school ja reha hai?
In Hindi: Kya Ram school ja rha hai?


Present perfect Tense

simple sentence

Ends with dita hai, kita hai and liya hai in Punjabi and kiya hai, liya hai and diya hai in hindi.

RULES:

: has / have+ 3rd form of verb

  • has -singular
  • have - plural

Example:
In English: Ram has gone to school.
In Punjabi: Ram school chala gya hai.
In Hindi: Ram school chala gya hai.

Negative sentence

RULES:

: has not /have not + 3st form of verb

  • Has not - singular
  • Have not- plural

Example:
In English:: Ram has not gone to school
In Punjabi: Ram school nahi gya hai
In Hindi: Ram school nahi gya hai

Interrogative sentence

RULES:

has/have before noun + 3st form of verb

Example:
In English: Has ram gone to school?
In punjabi: ki Ram school chala gya hai?
In Hindi: Kya Ram school chala gya hai?


Present perfect continuous Tense

simple sentence

Ends with reha hai, rahi hai and rahe han same as in present continuous. But the only difference is mentioning of time from which the action is being performed.

  • for: In case of exact time
  • since: Not exact time

RULES:

has been/have been + 1st form of verb + ing

  • has been-singular
  • have been- plural

Example:
In English: Ram has been going to school since 3 years
In Punjabi: Ram 3 sala to school ja reha hai.
In Hindi: Ram 3 saal se school ja rha hai.

Negative sentence

RULES:

: has not been /have not been + 3st form of verb

  • Has not been - singular
  • Have not been- plural

Example:
In English:: Ram has not been going to school since 3 years
In Punjabi: Ram 3 sala to school nahi ja reha hai
In Hindi: Ram 3 saal se school nahi ja rha hai.

Interrogative sentence

RULES:

has/have before noun + been + 1st form of verb +ing

Example:
In English: Has Ram been going to school since 3 years
In punjabi: ki Ram 3 sala to school ja reha hai?
In Hindi: Kya Ram 3 salon se school ja rha hai?
