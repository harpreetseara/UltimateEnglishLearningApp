Future Tense

It can be further divided into four categories.


Future Indefinite Tense:

simple sentence

These tenses end with ga, ge and gi in Punjabi and hindi.

RULES:

will/shall + 1st form of verb

  • will - singular
  • shall - plural

Example:
In English: Ram will go to school
In Punjabi: Ram School jaye ga
In Hindi: Ram school jaye ga

Negative sentence

RULES:

will not/shall not + first form of verb

Example:
In English: Ram will not go to school.
In Punjabi: Ram school nahi jaye ga.
In Hindi: Ram school nahi jaye ga.

Interrogative sentence

RULES:

will/shall before noun + first form of verb

Example:
In English: Will ram go to school?
In punjabi: ki Ram school jaye ga.
In Hindi: Kya Ram school jaye ga.


Future continuous Tense

simple sentence

Ends with reha hovega, rahi hovegi and rahe honge and rha hoga, rahi hogi in hindi.

RULES:

will be/shall be + 1st form of verb + ing

  • will be - singular
  • shall be - plural

Example:
In English: Ram will be going to school
In Punjabi: Ram school ja reha hovega
In Hindi: Ram school ja rha hoga.

Negative sentence

RULES:

: will not be / shall not be + 1st form of verb + ing

  • will not be - singular
  • shall not be - plural

Example:
In English: Ram will not be going to school.
In Punjabi: Ram school nahi ja reha hovega.
In Hindi: Ram school nahi ja rha hoga.

Interrogative sentence

RULES:

will/shall before noun + be + 1st form of verb + ing

Example:
In English: will ram be going to school?
In punjabi: ki Ram school ja reha hovega?
In Hindi: Kya Ram school ja rha hoga?


Future perfect Tense

simple sentence

Ends with dita hoyega, kita hoyega and liya hoyega in Punjabi and kiya hoga, liya hoga and diya hoga in hindi.

RULES:

: will have/shall have + 3rd form of verb

Example:
In English: Ram will have gone to school.
In Punjabi: Ram school chala gya hoyega.
In Hindi: Ram school chala gya hoga.

Negative sentence

RULES:

: will have not/shall have not + 3st form of verb

Example:
In English:: Ram will have not gone to school.
In Punjabi: Ram school nahi gya hoyega.
In Hindi: Ram school nahi gya hoga.

Interrogative sentence

RULES:

will/shall before noun + have + 3st form of verb

Example:
In English: will ram have gone to school?
In punjabi: ki Ram school chala hoyega?
In Hindi: Kya Ram school chala gya hoga?


Future perfect continuous Tense

simple sentence

Ends with reha hoyega, rahi hoyegi and rahe honge same as in Future continuous. But the only difference is mentioning of time from which the action is being performed.

  • for: In case of exact time
  • since: Not exact time

RULES:

will have been/shall have been + 1st form of verb + ing

Example:
In English: Ram will have been going to school since 3 years
In Punjabi: Ram 3 sala to school ja reha hovega.
In Hindi: Ram 3 saal se school ja rha hoga.

Negative sentence

RULES:

: will have not been/shall have not been + 3st form of verb

Example:
In English:: Ram will have not been going to school since 3 years
In Punjabi: Ram 3 sala to school nahi ja reha hoyega.
In Hindi: Ram 3 saal se school nahi ja rha hoga.

Interrogative sentence

RULES:

will/shall before noun + have been + 1st form of verb +ing

Example:
In English: Will Ram have been going to school since 3 years
In punjabi: ki Ram 3 sala to school ja reha hoyega?
In Hindi: Kya Ram 3 salon se school ja rha hoga?
